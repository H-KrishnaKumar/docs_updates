## Getting further tutorials
The purpose of the Getting Further series of tutorials is to show you advanced examples and use cases about different aspects of the platform. Code snippets and boilerplates are provided throughout the tutorials to make it easy and straightforward for you. Nonetheless, we recommend that you spend time exploring the platform’s options by yourself. Remember that you can refer to the Product Guide for an exhaustive description of each component of the platform at any time.
At this point, you should have set up an entire system that streamlines Chicago transit data from its sourcing to its visualization. If you haven’t, we suggest you go through our Getting Started guide and set up your first application. This app will be the cornerstone for the more advanced examples we are about to guide you through.
If you are ready to step up, check out the available guides below!
### Customize your dashboards further
🕒 Estimated duration: 1 hour
In this extension of the Getting Started guide, learn how to customize the visual aspects of your application.
{Customize your app further 📊}(/#/en/getting-further/app-manager/index.md)
### Code your own analytics application
🕒 Estimated duration: 2-4 hours
In this advanced step-by-step guide, learn how to create your own components and set up the charts, extensions and much more, so they best fit your application.
{Develop your own app 🖥}(/#/en/getting-further/app-dev/index.md)
### Do live predictions with your machine learning model!
🕒 Estimated duration: 1-2 hours
In this hands-on example, learn how to make the most of the Data Processing Engine and the Machine Learning component to create a live prediction model. Collecting live API weather data, you will set up a pipeline in a production environment to predict future Chicago transit figures and display insights on your app.
{Deploy and visualize your predictions 🤖}(#/en/getting-further/ml/index.md)
### Optimize computing power with segmentation!
🕒 Estimated duration: 1 hour
In this short guide, let us walk you through the job segmentation feature so you can execute your DPE jobs in a fraction of the time.
{Accelerate your pipelines with segmentation 🏎}(/#/en/getting-further/segmentation/dwh-attributes.md)
